Gold vs. Silver: Which Precious Metal Should You Buy?

6 min read • GoldBuller Research

The gold vs. silver question is the most common one new precious metals buyers ask. The honest answer: they serve different purposes and the best choice depends on your budget, goals, and storage capacity.

The Core Case for Gold

  • Store of value over millennia
  • Compact wealth storage: $100,000 in gold at $2,300/ozt weighs ~3 lbs. The same value in silver weighs ~237 lbs.
  • Lower premiums as a % of spot
  • Better volatility profile

The Core Case for Silver

  • Lower entry point: Under $35 per coin vs. $2,400+ for gold
  • Industrial demand tailwind: Solar panels, EV batteries, 5G, medical devices
  • Higher potential upside when the gold-silver ratio compresses

GoldBuller's Recommendation

For most investors: start with gold for wealth preservation and add silver for upside exposure. A 70% gold / 30% silver allocation by value is a reasonable starting point.
